29 - Junkyard Magic

Description for the Jury

This year"s effigy was specifically designed for accessibility and quick deployment, which would provide participants (weather permitting) with more time to experience the effigy and the effigy field than we have had in previous years. We have a real chance to *play.* We would like to bring this project as an addition to the space, to create an experience across the field that isn"t only beautiful and enjoyable, but continues the idea of rapid deployment and accessibility (as much as we can in an outdoor space). This project is designed to directly compliment the effigy, however, it is not part of the effigy- it simply extends the footprint and provides additional whimsical spaces for the community to explore. Cosmic Critter Carnival- imagine the intergalactic traffic jam that these cosmic critters caused on their way to the carnival. Even in space, critters don"t have drivers licenses! They don"t obey stellar traffic laws! Madness ensues, and now some of the UFOs are parked or totaled in epic fashion across the field. There"s occasional hissing, and lights, and led fires, and smoke bellowing from the wreckage. You might see a critter or three trying to get their bearings and yelling on the phone at their insurance company. The UFOs, critters, and other physical elements will be fabricated using quarter inch steel rod for the skeletal framework, with painted fabric over the shell (think Chinese Lanterns, but instead of wood and paper, we"re going for metal and fabric). LEDs will be installed in each piece, to capture the glowing "lantern" effect. Minimum 3 "crashed" UFOs will be build, an assortment of critters, and "flaming" (LED) fires dotting the field and firefly lights to add some magic. These pieces will be designed so that they may be enjoyed by all ages, anytime, day or night. The dimensions will vary per piece, however, the main UFOs will be 6ft diameter, and about 4ft tall. With multiple lit up pieces, this should help make the effigy field more inviting for participants to play on. Prepare to be transported to an intergalactic"crash site!

TimeLine

We are currently in the planning phase now. We have already sourced the metal we want to use and have the tools to tack weld it together. We have identified several sources for fabrics and LEDs. We intend to begin build the moment we know if there will be available funds for the project. These pieces are being designed to be modular, so that once we are on site, they will be quickly deployed (once we have permission to set up in the field, once DaFT is done with heavy machinery) and staked into place.
