6 - The VOID

Description for the Jury

The VOID is a massive, fancy kaleidoscope. People look in the end of what appears to be a big telescope and experience a spectacular light show of shifting colors and geometric shapes, reflected ad infinitum though vast space that appears to go on forever. What makes The VOID more intense than a regular kaleidoscope is three things: 1) Size: The body tube is 16 inches in diameter and 6 feet long. You can put your whole face up to it, giving you binocular vision into the illusion. 2) Geometry: Unlike a standard kaleidoscope, the mirrors are set at precise angles that cause tons of internal reflection, giving the impression of a distant, expanding space. 3) Lighting: Also unlike a standard kaleidoscope, one side has shapes etched through the mirror coating and is backlit by controllable LED strips. When this light bounces around the angled mirrors, it appears as infinitely reflected, animated geometric shapes. The sum total of the above creates an illusion that feels a bit like witnessing the birth of a universe. A spiky starburst shape in the middle of the view flickers and shimmers while waves of colored shapes and light expand from it, washing outwards over the viewer and into infinite space. From a practial perspective, this will be a large cylindrical object mounted on a wooden frame. Design and installation precautions will be taken to ensure it is robust against wind, rain, and people. It is not intended to be burned; it will be transported in and transported out of the event. This company makes small versions of what I'm planning as a reference: https://budderscopes.com/gallery/
